What is Microblading?

Microblading is a type of semi-permanent makeup where pigment is implanted into the second layer of skin through a manual process creating hairlike strokes.

The artist carefully designs your brows based on you your facial structure, precise measurements, natural brow hair, and your desired look.

This dexterous technique gives the illusion of real hair. The effects last from one to three years, but it is recommended to have an annual color boost to keep your brows looking fresh.

The pain is minimal -- most clients say it feels like threading many hairs. The area is numbed with a topical lidocaine numbing cream along with a medical grade numbing gel throughout the procedure.

Healing takes about two weeks. A free touch-up appointment will be fulfilled four to six weeks after the initial treatment.

Before Treatment

Do not take fish oil, vitamin E, aspirin or ibuprofen 72 hours before procedure (thins the blood).
Do not drink alcohol 24 hours before procedure.
Do not consume coffee before procedure.
Do not exercise the day of the procedure.
Do not wax or tint your eyebrows 3 days before procedure.
Do not tan two weeks prior or have a sunburned face.
Do not have any type of facials or chemical peels 2 weeks prior.
Discontinue Vitamin A (Retinol) products one month prior to treatment.
Do not have botox 3 weeks prior.
Must be off the prescription Accutane for ONE year prior to treatment.


Let any scabbing or flaky skin naturally exfoliate away.
DO NOT PICK OR SCRATCH SCAB. Picking can cause scarring or loss of pigment in the skin.
No exercise or heavy sweating for one week after treatment.
No direct water pressure on eyebrows in the shower or very hot/steamy showers for ten days.
No makeup or sunscreen on area.
Avoid swimming, hot tubs, and long showers for ten days.
No direct sun exposure or tanning for 4 weeks after procedure.
Use a fresh pillow case and avoid sleeping on your face for ten days.
No facials, botox, chemical treatments or microdermabrasion for 4 weeks.

After Treatment

Do not touch or wet area the day of procedure.
On days 1-7 gently wash morning and night, with an antibacterial soap to remove bacteria.

WASH HANDS before cleansing brows.

With a very light touch, use your fingertips to gently cleanse the eyebrows. Rub the area in a smooth motion for ten seconds and rinse with water ensuring that all soap is rinsed away.

To dry, gently PAT, not rub, with a clean tissue or gauze.

Do not use any cleansing products containing acids (glycolic, lactic, or AHA), or any exfoliants.
After area is dry, apply a rice grain amount of healing ointment (Aquaphor) to each eyebrow using a clean cotton swab and spread it across area.

Be sure not to over apply the ointment as this will suffocate the skin and delay healing. The ointment should be a thin, and barely noticeable on the skin.

Healing Process and What to Expect

The healing process for microblading varies for every client. Every clients body and skin reacts to the procedure different and proper aftercare will give best results.

Common Side Effects:

Red or pink tenderness in the area.

Mild swelling.

Moderate scabbing 3-5 days after the procedure.

Scabs will start to flake off between days 5-10.

DO NOT PICK OFF. Allow them to fall off naturally.

Eyebrows will appear very dark until the scabs naturally flake off.

After scabs are off, the pigment might look light, grey, or blurry.

In four weeks after the procedure is fully healed, the pigment will return to full color
